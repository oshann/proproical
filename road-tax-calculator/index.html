How This Is Calculated

  • Petrol/diesel: a flat fee applies up to 1,600cc, then a base rate plus a per-cc surcharge for every cc above each bracket's floor. Saloon (sedan/hatchback/coupe) and non-saloon (SUV/MPV/pickup) use different tables -- non-saloons pay slightly more at small engine sizes but noticeably less at large engine sizes.
  • Electric vehicles (EV): from 1 January 2026, JPJ taxes EVs by motor power output (kW) instead of engine cc, across power bands with no saloon/non-saloon distinction. Figures here are interpolated from the published band boundaries and are approximate -- confirm your exact figure via MyJPJ.
  • Sabah/Sarawak: East Malaysia rates are lower than Peninsular Malaysia. This calculator applies an approximate discount to the Peninsular formula; the official East Malaysia schedule may differ bracket by bracket.
  • Disclaimer: this calculator is a planning guide only and does not replace your official JPJ / MyJPJ renewal notice.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Malaysia road tax calculated for petrol/diesel cars?

A flat fee applies up to 1,600cc, then a base rate plus a per-cc surcharge above each bracket's floor, up to 3,000cc and beyond. Saloon and non-saloon vehicles use different tables.

Is EV road tax cheaper than petrol car road tax?

For most mainstream EVs, yes -- road tax is based on motor power (kW) instead of engine cc from 1 January 2026, with no saloon/non-saloon distinction.

Why is road tax cheaper in Sabah and Sarawak?

JPJ sets lower rates for East Malaysia given road conditions and reliance on larger 4WD vehicles. This calculator applies an approximate discount -- check MyJPJ for your exact figure.
